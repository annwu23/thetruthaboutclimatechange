Why Should I Care?

Climate change: it's real. But why should we care about climate change? What are the actual effects that everyday people will experience?





  1. Weather
    As mentioned on the "Effects" page, climate change will make weather worse. In the late 21st century, people living in some places of the world (such as near the Persian Gulf) might have to cope with days so hot that stepping outside could kill; even if you don't live there, practically every single part of the world can be hit by hurricanes, floods, or droughts worsened by climate change.

  2. Food and Water
    It's not happening yet, but with the rate at which climate change is happening over the world and a booming population, we could be faced with food problems very soon. Droughts make it harder to find water in some places of the world already, and some plants that we regularly eat for food — corn and rice, for example — might suffer. Meanwhile, many animals are going onto the endangered list, and soon their ecosystems will experience a huge imbalance in the food chain… which, of course, affects us.

  3. Rising Seas
    The rising ocean levels are arguably one of the biggest threats climate change poses. Antarctica alone can add 1.5 to 3.5 feet to the oceans, and when we add Greenland and other large glaciers in, that number could rise to 3~7 feet. This number might not seem like much, but more than 90 coastal communities are struggling with "chronic flooding" (where the flooding is so intolerable that people are evacuating) already. In less than 20 years, that statistic will rise to 170, and by the end of the century, 670 (including Cambridge, Massachusetts; Oakland, California; and New York City).

  4. Pollution
    Climate change is not directly linked to pollution, but both originate from the same cause: carbon dioxide emissions. The pollution coming from factories, cars, etc. is breathed in almost every time you step outside, and can be linked to lung cancer (though smoking is a greater cause).
